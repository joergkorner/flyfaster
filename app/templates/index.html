See why the fast pilots are fast.

Send us the day's IGC tracks — of a competition task or a shared XC day — and you get back two reports: how every pilot compares with the field on the levers that drive cross-country speed, and how each pilot's profile develops across days. Everything is measured from the tracks alone.

Upload your tracks →

Every pilot gets a profile

Real profiles from one flying day (10 pilots, names removed). Eight axes, each relative to the field of that day: the dashed line is the day median, the outer circle the day's best. Seven of the axes are speed levers — measurably linked to average speed — one (Flies far) describes style. A spoke touching the outer circle was that day's best in that lever.

What you get by email

Day view

One card per flight: speed, distance, radar profile, glide-speed distribution (wind removed), glide ratio and turns per km — the whole field side by side, day by day. Plus the day's wind, reconstructed from everyone's circling, and a statistics section showing which levers actually correlated with speed.

Pilot view

For each pilot, all day profiles in chronological order plus an average card — making visible how a profile develops over a season. Shown for every pilot with at least three flights in your upload.

How to prepare your zip

One folder per flying day, named YEAR_MONTH_DAY, each containing that day's .igc files. Zip the folders together — one day or a whole season:

my-tracks.zip
├── 2026_06_18/            ← a normal XC day
│   ├── anna.igc
│   ├── ben.igc
│   └── …
├── 2026_06_21_UTC1000/    ← a race: start time in the name,
│   └── …                     everything before 10:00 UTC is ignored
└── 2026_07_02/
    └── …

The comparison lives on shared conditions: the more pilots of the same day you include, the more the reports can say. Single-pilot uploads work but show no field to compare against.
